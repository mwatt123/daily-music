Broken Record — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026

Broken Record is a Chrome extension that replaces the new tab page with one hand-picked album recommendation per day, and lets you keep the ones you like into a personal on-device collection. This policy explains exactly what it does and does not do with your data.

What we collect

Nothing that leaves your device. The extension stores two things locally, using chrome.storage.local:

  • A single random, anonymous identifier that keeps your daily album pick stable across new tabs on a given day (and rotates it the next day).
  • Your crate — the albums you choose to keep, saved as their title, artist, year, and cover-image address so your collection survives browser restarts.

Both are stored only in your browser profile. Neither is ever transmitted to the developer or to any third party, and neither leaves your device.

No tracking or analytics

Broken Record contains no analytics, telemetry, advertising, or tracking of any kind. It does not collect your browsing history, personal information, or activity. No user data is sold or shared with anyone.

Album artwork and listening

Album cover images are loaded directly from third-party image services (Apple/iTunes and Amazon) purely to display the artwork. As with loading any image on the web, those services receive standard request information such as your IP address. The extension itself sends them no personal data.

Each album offers a Listen link. Following it simply opens Apple Music in your browser, exactly like clicking any ordinary web link; the extension sends no personal data when you do.

Permissions

The extension requests a single permission, storage, used only to save the anonymous identifier and your kept-album crate described above.
