Broken Record — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 July 2026

Broken Record is a Chrome extension that replaces the new tab page with one hand-picked album recommendation per day. This policy explains exactly what it does and does not do with your data.

What we collect

Nothing that leaves your device. The extension generates a single random, anonymous identifier and stores it locally using chrome.storage.local. That identifier exists only to keep your daily album pick stable across new tabs on a given day (and to rotate it the next day). It is never transmitted to the developer or to any third party, and it never leaves your browser profile.

No tracking or analytics

Broken Record contains no analytics, telemetry, advertising, or tracking of any kind. It does not collect your browsing history, personal information, or activity. No user data is sold or shared with anyone.

Album artwork

Album cover images are loaded directly from third-party image services (Apple/iTunes and Amazon) purely to display the artwork. As with loading any image on the web, those services receive standard request information such as your IP address. The extension itself sends them no personal data.

Permissions

The extension requests a single permission, storage, used only to save the anonymous identifier described above.
